Bio

I started front-end web dev during the first dot-com boom, back in the days when we laid out pages using rigid HTML tables . I progressed quickly to writing JavaScript and CSS along with using Photoshop to optimize images for the web. With my appetite whetted, I soon began to ask the back-end developers about the CGI scripts they were writing in Perl, I learned the concepts of CGI and using Unix commands to deploy code to our servers.

A change of employer introduced me to the world of Microsoft Active Server Pages (classic ASP) and soon I was creating web applications using ASP in Homesite / Dreamweaver. I learned how to interact with SQL Server databases using ADO, I became a skilled ASP developer and was a key member of the team that developed and launched a full-scale eLearning portal.

Wanderlust lead me to South-East Asia and the Antipodes and a career-break. Upon my return to Europe, the dot-com bubble had burst and it was time to return to full-time education. In quick succession I completed the following;

  • Level 5 Certificate in Creative Digital Media Production
  • Level 6 Advanced Certificate in Software Development (equivilant to an Associate Degree)
  • Sun Certified Programmer for the Java 2 Platform 1.2
I then completed an internship with a company who were so impressed with my performance that they immediately offered me a full-time position developing web applications for government agencies. It was here that I first encountered ASP.NET, C# and the .NET Framework. Eventually an opportunity arose for me to work in Microsoft as a contract QA Tester. Working in a Scrum team was a huge eye-opener for me and convinced me that this is the most effective methodology for development teams who are constantly releasing new features.

When my contract with Microsoft expired, I returned to the role of Web Applications Developer, this time working for an ecommerce company. The web team were responsible for delivering content to 17 countries in 11 different languages! I spent three happy years with this company until I felt it was time to experience life on another continent.

Upon my arrival in Canada I quickly found employment as a web developer in the online gaming sector. Working closely with the creative director, I was responsible for creating standards-compliant web templates which I then imported into the Telerik Sitefinity CMS. When that employer decided to relocate I was recruited as a Technical Project Manager with one of the largest online promotional companies in North America. For 3 years I liaised with Fortune 500 clients to establish their requirements, conveyed these requirements to the design and development teams, allocated resources and ensured the timely completion of all projects.

Some time in 2014 I decided that it was time to finally complete a Bachelor of Technology in Computing that I had previously started at Dublin Institute of Technology, as of July 2016 I have passed all my exams and, with one final item of coursework to submit, I fully expect to be conferred in late 2016. In addition to that, I have recently become a Microsoft Certified Professional having successfully passed the 70-480 Programming in HTML5 with JavaScript and CSS3 exam, the first step to my goal of becoming a Microsoft Certified Solutions Developer in Web Applications.

In my spare time I love to Snowboard and take any opportunity I get to enjoy the North Shore mountains, Whistler and further afield. I am a qualified CASI Level 1 Snowboard Instructor and during the 2015/2016 winter season I volunteered with Vancouver Adaptive Snow Sports, a wonderful charity organisation dedicated to making the mountain accessible for all. I attempt to play rugby with the East Vancouver Scribes, and I enjoy living life to the fullest in Beautiful BC.
